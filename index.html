Gender Employment Share by Country, Income Group, and Year

This is an interactive view of male vs. female employment shares by country and year, shown for selected employment types and income groups derived from data collected by The World Bank, an organization that plays a vital role in collecting internationally comparable statistics about global development. Countries are ordered by most even to most lopsided gender split. Additionally box-plots display the overall distribution shares for male and female employed populations from the filters selected.

The types of employment are as follows:

  • Wage and salaried workers: People who work for an employer and are paid wages or salaries.
  • Employment in services: Workers employed in service-sector jobs like retail, education, healthcare, finance, and public services.
  • Employment in industry: Workers employed in industry, including manufacturing, construction, mining, and utilities.
  • Contributing family workers: People who work in a family business or farm without formal pay.
  • Vulnerable employment: Workers in less secure jobs, typically own-account workers and contributing family workers.
  • Self-employed: People who work for themselves rather than for an employer.
  • Employers: Self-employed people who run a business and hire others.
  • Employment in agriculture: Workers employed in farming, forestry, fishing, and related agricultural activity.

It is important to note that types of employment are not mutually exclusive.
